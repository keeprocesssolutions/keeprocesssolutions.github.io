Meet the Founder

Hayden Kee

Hayden Kee brings over a decade of experience in project management, process development, and process improvement across a wide range of industries, from construction and agriculture to manufacturing, analytical laboratories, R&D, applied technology, and process technology development.

Raised in Modesto, California, Hayden has deep roots in the Central Valley's agricultural community. That upbringing instilled a practical, hands-on perspective that shapes everything about how Kee Process Solutions operates today.

"Every business — large or small — has untapped potential waiting to be unlocked. My goal is to help organizations become more resilient, efficient, and sustainable by improving process flow and developing the people who run those processes every day."

Hayden's expertise spans Lean Six Sigma, Scrum, and classic project management methodologies. Rather than forcing rigid frameworks onto your operation, the focus is always on tailoring continuous improvement solutions that fit your actual needs and the people doing the work.

Our Framework

The Weather-Proofing Method™

A proprietary five-step framework — simple, practical, flexible, and people-centered — designed for real-world operations.

01 — Forecast

Identify vulnerabilities and sources of uncertainty before they become crises.

02 — Fortify

Redesign workflows to eliminate fragility and reduce single points of failure.

03 — Flow

Establish cadence and smooth the progression of work across your operation.

04 — Flex

Build adaptive capacity through cross-training and clear decision frameworks.

05 — Monitor

Create visual controls and early-warning systems so issues surface before they escalate.

Why This Approach Works

The Weather‑Proofing Method is effective because it's:

  • Simple — easy to teach, easy to use
  • Practical — built for real operations, not theory
  • Flexible — works in agriculture, construction, manufacturing, labs, and beyond
  • People‑centered — builds capability, not dependency
  • Resilient — designed for the unpredictable nature of real work

This method reflects everything I've learned across construction, agriculture, labs, manufacturing, and process technology development. It's not about forcing your operation into a rigid framework — it's about building a system that fits the way you work and helps your team thrive.
